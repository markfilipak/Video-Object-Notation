DIGITAL CAMERA: Suppose an ARRI Alexa XT shoots a 16:9 'PAL' video.

1712 x 1101 is the number of RG+GB (2−by−2 square) photo sites in the Alexa's Bayer sensor array.

2242 x 1441 is the number of RGB-equivalent sample points synthesized from the photo sites.

2240 x 1260 sample points is the largest (i.e., highest resolution) 16:9 capture area.

 720 x 576  pixels is the target 16:9 'PAL' picture.

FILM SCANNER: If samples are made by film scanning, CPA is calculated from Δp & Δr.

Example:

scan plan: 21.31 x 18.16 mm is the 2.34 aspect CinemaScope, 35mm projection film frame.

scan plan:  1024 x 576 is the target 16:9 'PAL' display.

scan plan:  1024 x 436 is the display area for 2.34 aspect.

scan plan:   720 x 436 is the picture area for 2.34 aspect.

scan plan:  1440 x 872 is the Nyquist samples.

scan set−up: Δp = (21.31 mm−per−row)/(1440 pixels−per−row) = 14.7 µm−per−pixel.

scan set−up: Δr =         (18.16 mm)/(872 rows)            = 20.8 µm−per−row.

                             FILM SCANNER ——————————>   PICTURE ——————————————>   PIXELS to DOTS ——————>   DISPLAY                
Δp = 14.7 µm
Δr = 20.8 µm		 720 ppr
576 rpp (matted)		 1024/720 = 64/45 dpp
1 lpr		 1024 dpl
576 lpd
SAR = 69/49  DAR = 1024/576 = 16/9 

[note 2]

1, If displayed with 69/49 SAR, a 720 x 436 picture would produce a true-aspect, 1013 x 436 display image but unfortunately 69/49 SAR is not allowed.

2, On the other hand, if displayed using the required 64/45 SAR for 16:9 'PAL' DVDs, a 720 x 436 picture would instead produce a display image that's stretched by 11 dots (to 1024 x 436).

Thus, the 16:9 'PAL' DVD could be mastered,

1, with 11 dots of left-right matte added to the display image, or

2, as a slightly stretched display image with no left-right matte.

Though option 2's slight stretching (1%) would be unnoticeable, most shot-on-film DVD authors take option 1. However, there's a problem with option 1. In order to add 11 dots of matte to the display, 8 pixels of matte must be added to the pictures. But since the pictures are already 720 ppr, those 8 pixels can't simply be added because 728 ppr is not allowed for DVDs. So, the 8 pixels have to be figured into the scan plan from the beginning by reducing 1440 x 872 Nyquist to 1424 x 864 Nyquist for example. But the reduction to 1424 Nyquist will change the 69/49 SAR to a different SAR. When rescanned at the new aspect, the result will be closer but still not optimal. As seen in many shot-on-film cases, the 1013 film scan pixels are often simply matted out to 1024 even though the resulting image shows matting on all 4 sides and resolution is slightly degraded.
