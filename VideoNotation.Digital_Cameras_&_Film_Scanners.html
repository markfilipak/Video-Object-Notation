DIGITAL CAMERA: Suppose an ARRI Alexa XT shoots a 16:9 'PAL' video.

1712 x 1101 is the number of RG+GB (2−by−2 square) photo sites in the Alexa's Bayer sensor array.

2242 x 1441 is the number of RGB-equivalent sample points synthesized from the photo sites.

2240 x 1260 sample points is the largest (i.e., highest resolution) 16:9 capture area.

 720 x 576  samples is the target 16:9 'PAL' picture.

FILM SCANNER: If samples are made by film scanning, SPX is calculated from Δp & Δr.

Example:

scan plan: 21.31 x 18.16 mm is the CinemaScope 2.34 aspect, 35mm projection film frame.

scan plan:  1024 x 576 is the target 16:9 'PAL' display.

scan plan:  1024 x 437 is the display area for 2.34 aspect.

scan plan:   720 x 437 is the picture area for 2.34 aspect.

scan plan:  1440 x 874 is the Nyquist samples.

scan set−up: Δp = (21.31 mm−per−row)/(1440 pixels−per−row) = 14.7 µm−per−pixel.

scan set−up: Δr =         (18.16 mm)/(874 rows)            = 20.7 µm−per−row.

[note 2]

1, If displayed with 69/49 SAR, a 720 x 437 picture would produce a true-aspect, 1013 x 437 display image, but unfortunately, 69/49 SAR is not allowed.

2, On the other hand, if displayed using the required 64/45 for 16:9 'PAL' DVDs, a 720 x 437 picture would instead produce a display image that's stretched by 11 dots (to 1024 x 437).

Thus, this 16:9 'PAL' DVD could be mastered,

1, with 11 dots of left-right matte added to the display image, or

2, as a slightly stretched display image with no left-right matte.

Though option 2's slight stretching (1%) would be unnoticeable, most shot-on-film DVD authors take option 1. However, there's a problem with option 1. In order to add 11 dots of matte to the display, 8 pixels of matte must be added to the pictures. But since the pictures are already 720 ppr, those 8 pixels can't simply be added because 728 ppr is not allowed for DVDs. So, the 8 pixels have to be figured into the scan plan from the beginning by reducing 1440 x 874 Nyquist to 1424 x 864 Nyquist for example. But the reduction to 1424 Nyquist will change the 49/69 SAR to a different aspect with different rounding. When rescanned at the new aspect, the result will be closer but still not correct. Often, the 8 pixels are simply cropped from the pictures.
