Terminology & Intrinsic Rates & Default MPEG Tagging
System architects & documentarians recommend that unique data types be given unique names.

  Key: new terms (but not used in notations)     stream names (used in notations)
Uniqueness Principle and Assigning Names
The naming principle aims to limit the number of element & stream names as much as possible.

1, Differentiation of features should be explicit, never implied.

2, Differentiation of features should never be based on context or application or textual descriptions.

3, With one exception, differentiation must not refer to the properties of (or even the existence of) other features, no matter how closely related.

3.1, The exception: Defining parts and then defining the assembly of those parts as an assembly of the parts is okay. For example, suppose C is a combination of A plus B. Defining A and B as the A'th and B'th parts of C is not okay even if C is defined in advance, but defining A and B and then defining C as A plus B is okay.
The naming principle is this:

 • If AV items can be given identical names and then differentiated via notation, then that's what should be done.

 • Otherwise:

 • Items are given unique names unless the difference is just image properties like pixel values, width, height, and the like — image properties can be set via dotted properties and methods.

 • Streams are given unique names unless the difference is just element counts [see Example 1].


Example 1:
t1r0frames t1r1frames t0r0frames and t0r1frames (defined in the sections that follow) have individual names because each has unique 'top_field_first' & 'repeat_first_field' MPEG tags. In contrast, 'PAL'-soft streams and 'NTSC'-soft streams made from them are not considered unique, and are grouped together under the name "stframes". They can share the same name because they are populated by the same sequence of the same elements: 
  'PAL'-soft stream
  11 t1r0frames + 1 t1r1frame + 11 t0r0frames + 1 t0r1frame + ..  24 PAL stframes per stride

  'NTSC'-soft stream
   1 t1r0frame  + 1 t1r1frame +  1 t0r0frame  + 1 t0r1frame + ..  4 NTSC stframes per stride
The only difference between 'PAL'-soft versus 'NTSC'-soft streams is the varying numbers of t#r#frames they contain. Since the notation smoothly handles varying numbers, 'PAL'-soft versus 'NTSC'-soft streams are not considered unique and therefore do not deserve unique names.

Example 2:
Before pull down, streams of halfpics have no pattern, just structurally identical halfpics. After pull down, streams of halfpics are still structurally identical, so still don't deserve to be given unique names. But once the halfpics are framed, the resulting frames do structurally differ and the streams show patterns. Consequently, they are given unique names: "ftframes" versus "combedtframes". 
  'PAL'-hard stream
  12 ftframes + 12 combedtframes + 1 ftframe + ..  25 PAL htframes per stride

  'NTSC'-hard stream
   2 ftframes +  2 combedtframes + 1 ftframe + ..  5 NTSC htframes per stride

progressive: The word "progressive" is used here in the informal sense. Informally, "progressive" means a frame in which all lines are concurrent (which includes picframes ftframes & stframes), whereas formally, "progressive" means bearing an MPEG 'progressive_frame'=1 tag (which is limited to picframes).

unweave: To make a 'top' (or 'bottom') half-picture, by extracting the odd (or even) lines of an existing picture.

interlace: To make a pseudo picture by alternating the lines of two scans or non-progressive halfpics.

pseudo picture: Having the appearance of a picture but non-progressive due to line-to-line motion combing.

telecine: To apply pull down to an hpps stream or to tag it for later pull down.

Scans
Raw scans are images shot by analog TV cameras.
PAL-scans & NTSC-scans are streams of field-encoded scans that are found on Blu-rays & DVDs.

sps: scans per second. Raw scans, DAR cropped or matted & 1/(SAR)-scaled.

 • Stream appearance: 1/(SAR)-scaled, half height, no combed lines, no cadence or judder.

 • Intrinsic rate: Set in the camera. Usually either PAL (50sps) or NTSC (60'1.001sps).

 • Defaults:

 •       50sps: .spr="768"   .rpc="288"   .colormatrix="bt601"

 • 60'1.001sps: .spr="640"   .rpc="240"   .colormatrix="bt601"

sfps: scanframes per second. Frames of field-encoded scans.

 • Intrinsic rate: (sps)/2.

 • Defaults:

 •       50sfps: .ppr="720"   .rpp="288"   .colormatrix="bt601"

 • 60'1.001sfps: .ppr="720"   .rpp="240"   .colormatrix="bt601"

 • Default MPEG tagging:

 • tbscanframes: 'progressive_frame'=0, 'top_field_first'=1, 'repeat_first_field'=0.

 • btscanframes: 'progressive_frame'=0, 'top_field_first'=0, 'repeat_first_field'=0.

spps: scanpics per second. Interlaced scans (pseudo pictures).

 • Stream appearance: 1/(SAR)-scaled, full height, moderate motion combing (1/(sps) seconds, odd-to-even lines), no cadence or judder.

 • Strides: 2 scans —> 1 scanpic.

 • Intrinsic rate: (sps)/2.

spfps: scanpicframes per second. Frame-encoded scanpics (non-progressive but tagged as progressive).

 • Intrinsic rate: spps.

 • Default MPEG tagging: 'progressive_frame'=1, 'top_field_first'=0, 'repeat_first_field'=0.

scan:

field of view: Full.

horizontal resolution: Full.

vertical resolution: Half.

pixel aspect: SAR.

image aspect: 2x(DAR)x(SAR).

row-to-row interval: Rows are consecutive, top to bottom, spanning 1/(sps) seconds [see note].

pixel-to-pixel interval: Pixels are consecutive, left to right, spanning the row-to-row interval [see note].


[note] Ignoring relatively brief track-to-track (horizontal) & scan-to-scan (vertical) blanking intervals.

 • A pixels-by-lines data set made from a raw scan via (DAR)x2 cropping or matting followed by SAR scaling.

 • Image appearance: 1/(SAR)-scaled, half height, no combed lines, slight diagonal motion parallax from top-left pixel to bottom-right pixel that can (usually unnoticeably) 'bend' straight lines.

scanframes (scan frames): A stream of tbscanframes or btscanframes but not a mix of the two.

tbscanframe ('top'-'bottom' scan frame): A consecutive pair of field-encoded scans in which the 'top' scan comes first.

btscanframe ('bottom'-'top' scan frame): A consecutive pair of field-encoded scans in which the 'bottom' scan comes first.

scanpic (scan picture):

field of view: Full.

horizontal resolution: Full.

vertical resolution: Full.

pixel aspect: SAR.

image aspect: (DAR)x(SAR).

line-to-line interval: Lines from the 1st scan are interleaved with lines from the 2nd scan;
line(2 3..)time = line(1 2..)time ± 1/(sps) seconds.

pixel-to-pixel interval: The same as for scans.

 • The pseudo picture formed by interlacing a pair of consecutive scans.

 • Image appearance: 1/(SAR)-scaled, full height, moderate motion combing (1/(sps) seconds, each odd to even numbered line).

scanpicframe (scan picture frame): A frame-encoded scanpic.


[note] The meaning of 'top_field_first' varies.

 • For scanframes, 'top_field_first' is descriptive. It answers the question: Which field comes first in the frame? The 'repeat_first_field' tag is ignored.

 • For stframes, 'top_field_first' is prescriptive. It answers the question: How is pull down done during play? Internally, stframes are always 'top' followed by 'bottom'. The 'top_field_first' & 'repeat_first_field' tags are instead used to control the pull down that is to be done during play.

Pictures

Cine-slow are streams of frame-encoded pictures (often called "progressive") that are found on Blu-rays but not on DVDs.

Field telecines
Telecines are pictures adapted for analog TV. Each original picture is unweaved to two halfpics and field-encoded during mastering.
PAL-fast (aka "PAL speedup") streams are found on Blu-rays & DVDs. For field telecine, field tags are not configured for pull down during either mastering or play — there is no pull down at all.

 raw captures —> DAR —> 1/(SAR) —> pps —> unweave —> hpps —> fields —> ftfps —> media
pps: pictures per second. Raw captures, DAR cropped or matted & 1/(SAR)-scaled.

 • Stream appearance: 1/(SAR)-scaled, full height, no combed lines, no cadence, no judder.

 • Intrinsic rate: Set in the camera. For cinema, usually 24pps.

hpps: halfpics per second. Half-pictures unweaved from pictures.

 • Stream appearance: 1/(SAR)-scaled, half height, no combed lines, no cadence, no judder.

 • Strides: 1 picture —> 2 halfpics.

 • Intrinsic rate: (pps)x2.

 • Intrinsic rate: (hpps)/2.

 • Default MPEG tagging: 'progressive_frame'=0, 'top_field_first'=1, 'repeat_first_field'=0.

Soft Telecines
NTSC-soft streams are found on Blu-rays & DVDs — 'PAL'-soft may also exist. For soft telecine, as for field telecine, halfpics are mastered as fields but unlike field telecine, pull-down tags are added during mastering so that pull down is done during play.

stfps: stframes per second. Frames of field-encoded halfpics (progressive, pull-down tagged).

 • Intrinsic rate: Copied from the media.

hpps: halfpics per second. Half-pictures unweaved from pictures.

 • Stream appearance: 1/(SAR)-scaled, half height, no combed lines, no cadence, no judder.

 • Intrinsic rate: (stfps)x2.

tpps: telepics per second. Interlaced halfpics (pseudo pictures).

 • Stream appearance: 1/(SAR)-scaled, full height, conspicuous motion combing (1/(pps) seconds, all odd lines to all even lines), pronounced cadence & judder.

 • Intrinsic rate: (hpps)/2.

stframe (soft telecined frame): An ftframe tagged so that players do pull down.

Hard Telecines
NTSC-hard streams are found on Blu-rays & DVDs — PAL-hard may also exist. For hard telecine, as for field & soft telecines, halfpics are mastered as fields but unlike soft telecine, pull down is done during mastering, so the number of fields is greater than the number of original halfpics by a pull-down factor: 48-to-50 for 'PAL', 8-to-10 for 'NTSC'.

pps: pictures per second. Raw captures, DAR cropped or matted & 1/(SAR)-scaled.

 • Stream appearance: 1/(SAR)-scaled, full height, no combed lines, no cadence, no judder.

 • Intrinsic rate: Set in the camera. For cinema, usually 24pps.

hpps: halfpics per second. Half-pictures unweaved from pictures.

 • Stream appearance: 1/(SAR)-scaled, half height, no combed lines, no cadence, no judder.

Left:

 • Strides: 1 picture —> 2 halfpics.

 • Intrinsic rate: (pps)x2.

Right:

 • Strides: Determined by stride-match notation. Example: For (AaBbCcDd)(AaBbBcCdDd), (8 halfpics) —> (10 halfpics).

 • Intrinsic rate: (left hpps)x(right stride)/(left stride). Example: (48hpps)x(10 halfpics)/(8 halfpics) = 60hpps.

htfps: htframes per second. Frames of field-encoded halfpics (non-progressive).

 • Intrinsic rate: (hpps)/2.

 • Default MPEG tagging:

 • ftframe: 'progressive_frame'=0, 'top_field_first'=1, 'repeat_first_field'=0.

 • combedtframe: 'progressive_frame'=0, 'top_field_first'=1, 'repeat_first_field'=0.

tpps: telepics per second. Interlaced halfpics (pseudo pictures).

 • Stream appearance: 1/(SAR)-scaled, full height, conspicuous motion combing, pronounced cadence & judder.

 • Intrinsic rate: (hpps)/2.

tpfps: telepicframes per second. Frame-encoded telepics (non-progressive but tagged as progressive).

 • Intrinsic rate: tpps.

 • Default MPEG tagging: 'progressive_frame'=1, 'top_field_first'=0, 'repeat_first_field'=0.
