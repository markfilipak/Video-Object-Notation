Tokens & Strides
Capitalization, e.g. A versus a, differentiates two differing tokens, but capitalization otherwise has no significance at all. Sequencing, e.g. AB versus BA versus wK, differentiates two differing tokens, but sequencing otherwise has no significance at all. All that matters is the order of tokens and matching. What are shown here is merely traditional usage.

Tokens within strides. 
0..9 signify temporary or undifferentiated elements.

  - (hyphen) in  sps streams is a wildcard signifying 1 scan. For example, -- signifies 2 consecutive scans.
             in  pps                                  1 picture.
             in hpps                                  1 halfpic.

A..Z in  sps streams signify top scans.    For example, AbCd
     in  sps                 top scans.                 aBcD
     in  pps                 pictures.                  A B
     in hpps                 top halfpics.              AaBb

a..z in  sps streams signify bottom scans. For example, AbCd
     in  sps                 bottom scans.              aBcD
     in  sps                 arbitrary scans.           abcd
     in  pps                 nothing.
     in hpps                 bottom halfpics.           AaBb
The information above may be more accessible via the following table.

Tokens in sps streams Depictions in diagrams [see note 1]

AbCd = 4 consecutive scans

(A)(b)(C)(d) = 4 consecutive scans, top field first

aBcD = 4 consecutive scans

(a)(B)(c)(D) = 4 consecutive scans, bottom field first

abcd = 4 consecutive scans

(a)(b)(c)(d) = 4 consecutive scans, arbitrary sequence

AC = 2 top scans of AbCd

(A)(C) = 2 scans decoded from [A+b_][C+d_]

BD = 2 top scans of aBcD

(B)(D) = 2 scans decoded from [a+B_][c+D_]

bd = 2 bottom scans of AbCd

(b)(d) = 2 scans decoded from [A+b_][C+d_]

ac = 2 bottom scans of aBcD

(a)(c) = 2 scans decoded from [a+B_][c+D_]


Tokens in pps streams Depictions in diagrams [see note 1]

AB = 2 consecutive pictures

 

(Aa__)(Bb__) = 2 pictures decoded from [Aa__][Bb__]


Tokens in hpps streams Depictions in diagrams [see note 1]

AaBb = 4 consecutive halfpics

(A)(a)(B)(b) = 4 halfpics unweaved from (Aa__)(Bb__)

AB = 2 top halfpics

(A)(B) = 2 halfpics extracted from (Aa__)(Bb__)

ab = 2 bottom halfpics

(a)(b) = 2 halfpics extracted from (Aa__)(Bb__)


[note 1] Diagrams appear solely to aid visualization but are not part of the notation.


Tokens that cross stride boundaries.

^ (caret) in the current stride is replaced by a copy of the matching stream element from the ending of the previous stride. For example, (Ab)(Ab(A^)) takes the 3 scan pair stream, AbCdEf, substitutes according to AbC^E^, and outputs AbCbEd.

$ (dollar sign) in the current stride is replaced by a copy of the matching stream element from the beginning of the next stride. For example, (Ab)(($b)Ab) takes the 3 scan pair stream, AbCdEf, substitutes according to $b$dEf, and outputs CbEdEf.
