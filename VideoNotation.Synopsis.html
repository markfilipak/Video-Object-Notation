Synopsis
This presentation's audience is experienced video engineers and coders.

Notations are lines of English text used to model video streams. They are always read from left to right. They represent time that always flows from left to right and is always continuous. Thus, to the left is upstream and to the right is downstream.

 • The notation is object-oriented. Streams & frames & fields & image elements are all objects.

 • Streams are represented by names. Image elements are represented by tokens.

 • Object editing is done via the object's properties & methods.

 • Each type of object has its own method defaults.

 • Pixel editing is done via the image element's properties & methods.

 • Twelve standard streams are identified by their physical characteristics and are given unique names.

 • Streams are traversed via strides of tokens that are characteristic of each stream type.

 • Video editing & transcoding are presented via textual descriptions & notations & o'scope-style diagrams.

 • Several new video editing and transcoding techniques are introduced.

PAL-scan is a 50sps stream that has been field encoded and framed at 25sfps.

NTSC-scan is a 60'1.001sps stream that has been field encoded and framed at 30'1.001sfps.

Cinema is a raw stream from a cinema camera or film scanner at 24pps.

Cine-slow is cinema that has been frame encoded and forced to 24'1.001pfps.

'PAL'-fast is cinema that has been unweaved, field encoded, framed, and forced to 25ftfps.

'NTSC'-soft is cinema that has been unweaved, field encoded, framed, forced to 24'1.001stfps, and tagged for 8-to-10 telecine.

'NTSC'-hard is cinema that has been unweaved, 8-to-10 telecined, field encoded, framed, and forced to 30'1.001htfps.

'PAL'-soft & 'PAL'-hard may exist but have not been seen in the wild.

'PAL'-soft is cinema that has been unweaved, field encoded, framed at 24stfps, and tagged for 48-to-50 telecine.

'PAL'-hard is cinema that has been unweaved, 48-to-50 telecined, field encoded, and framed at 25htfps.

scans sps

<— SAR-scaled captures at scans−per−second rate.

 • diagram elements: (A)(b) is 2 scans with 'top' scan first, (a)(B) is 2 scans with 'bottom' scan first.

scanframes sfps

<— scan frames, e.g. PAL-scan & NTSC-scan TV shows & some documentaries on DVDs & older blu-rays; 2 field-encoded scans per frame at scanframes−per−second rate.

 • diagram elements: [A+b][C+d] are 2 scanframes with 'top' field first, [a+B][c+D] are 2 scanframes with 'bottom' field first.

 • sfps[sps decodes a downstream sps stream, e.g. (a)(b)(c)(d).., from upstream scanframes, e.g. [a+b][c+d]...

 • sps]sfps encodes a downstream sfps stream, e.g. [a+b][c+d].., from upstream scans, e.g. (a)(b)(c)(d)..; drops final orphan, if any.

 • sps(ab)(((ab))a+a)]sfps same as previous except repeats final orphan, if any.

        sps                // (a___)(b___)(c___)(d___)..(y___) checked for orphan
        (ab)(((ab))a+a)    // (a___)(b___)(c___)(d___)..(y___)(y___) detected, repeated
        ]sfps              // [a+b_______][c+d_______]..[y+y_______] encoded

 • sps(a)(aa)]sfps is bob decomb.

        sps                // (A___)(b___)(C___)(d___)..(z___) orphans don't matter
        (a)(aa)            // (A)(A)(b)(b)(C)(C)(d)(d)..(z)(z) images & rate doubled
        ]sfps              // [A+A_][b+b_][C+C_][d+d_]..[z+z_] encoded

 • &="interpolate"sps(ab)((a&$b)a&bb)]sfps is SME decomb.

        &="interpolate"    // operator loaded
        sps(ab)(((ab))a+a) // (a___)(b___)(c___)(d___)..(y___)(z___) checked for orphan
        (ab)((a&$b)a&bb)   // (A&C_)(b___)(C&E_)(d___)..(Y&z_)(z___) interpolated
        ]sfps              // [B+b_______][D+d_______]..[Y&z+z_____] encoded
pictures pps

<— SAR-scaled captures at pictures−per−second rate.

 • diagram elements: (Aa__)(Bb__) are 2 pictures.

picframes pfps

<— picture frames, e.g. cine-slow blu-ray movies; frame-encoded pictures (aka progressive) at picframes−per−second rate.

 • diagram elements: [Aa__][Bb__] are 2 picframes.

 • pfps[pps decodes a downstream pps stream, e.g. (Aa__)(Bb__).., from upstream picframes, e.g. [Aa__][Bb__]...

 • pps]pfps encodes a downstream pfps stream, e.g. [Aa__][Bb__].., from upstream pictures, e.g. (Aa__)(Bb__)...

 • 24pps.fromfile("source").a.fromfile("audiosource").s.fromfile("subtitlesource")]24'1.001pfps.tofile("target") creates a cine-slow video. Since they are attached prior to encoding, audio & subs are automatically resampled by |24'1.001 pfps|/|24 pps| = 1000/1001 during encoding.

halfpics hpps

<— half-pictures at halfpics−per−second rate.

 • diagram elements: (A)(a)(B)(b) are 4 halfpics unweaved from 2 pictures, (Aa__)(Bb__).

 • hpps__pps weaves downstream pictures, e.g. (Aa__)(Bb__).., from upstream halfpics, e.g. (A)(a)(B)(b)...

 • pps__hpps unweaves downstream halfpics, e.g. (A)(a)(B)(b).., from upstream pictures, e.g. (Aa__)(Bb__)...

ftframes ftfps

<— field telecined frames, e.g. 'PAL'-fast DVD movies; progressive, concurrent halfpic pairs field-encoded & framed at ftframes−per−second rate.

 • all fields tagged 'progressive_frame'=0, 'top_field_first'=1, 'repeat_first_field'=0;

 • diagram elements: [A+a_][B+b_] are 2 ftframes.

 • ftfps[hpps: decodes a downstream hpps stream, e.g. (A)(a)(B)(b).., from upstream ftframes, e.g. [A+a_][B+b_]...

 • hpps]ftfps: encodes a downstream ftframe stream, e.g. [A+a_][B+b_].., from upstream halfpics, e.g. (A)(a)(B)(b)...

 • 24pps.fromfile("source")hpps]25ftfps.a.fromfile("audiosource").s.fromfile("subtitlesource").tofile("target") creates a 'PAL'-fast video. Since they are attached to 25ftfps, audio & subs are presumed to already match 25ftfps.

stframes stfps

<— soft telecined frames, e.g. 'PAL'-soft & 'NTSC'-soft DVD movies; progressive, but fields; the following frames at stframes−per−second rate:

11 ('PAL') or 1 ('NTSC') ftframes
plus 1 ftframes but tagged 'progressive_frame'=0, 'top_field_first'=1, 'repeat_first_field'=1.
plus 11 ('PAL') or 1 ('NTSC') ftframes but tagged 'progressive_frame'=0, 'top_field_first'=0, 'repeat_first_field'=0.
plus 1 ftframes but tagged 'progressive_frame'=0, 'top_field_first'=0, 'repeat_first_field'=1.

 • diagram elements: [A+a_][B+b_] are 2 stframes.

 • stfps__ftfps mux-converts to ftframes by setting all frames to 'progressive_frame'=0, 'top_field_first'=1, 'repeat_first_field'=0.

 • hpps]stfps encodes a downstream stframe stream, e.g. [A+a_][B+b_].., from upstream halfpics, e.g. (A)(a)(B)(b)...

 • 24pps.fromfile("source")hpps]24'1.001stfps.tofile("target") creates an 'NTSC'-soft video.

Note: Some DVD movies that are outwardly stfps appear to have some sections, usually coinciding with particular scenes, that have stretches of hard telecined frames 'underneath' the soft telecine. The sections have oddly spaced combed pairs sometimes separated by 5 uncombed frames, sometimes by 7, sometimes by 9, etc. For such situations, consider adding this notation fragment: &="blend"(ab)(ab.iscombed?a&bb:ab), somewhere downstream from the source decoding. The notation fragment half-blends the combed frames but retains a pleasing sharpness without softening the entire movie.

htframes htfps

<— hard telecined frames, e.g. 'PAL'-hard & 'NTSC'-hard DVD movies; the following frames at htframes−per−second rate:

13 ('PAL') or 3 ('NTSC')     ftframes
plus 12 ('PAL') or 2 ('NTSC') htcombframes

<— like ftframes but containing nonconcurrent halfpic pairs.

 • diagram elements: [A+a_][B+b_][B+c_][C+d_][D+d_] are 5 'NTSC' htframes with 2-3-2-3 pull-down. Frames 3 & 4 are htcombframes.

 • hpps]htfps encodes a downstream htframe stream, e.g. [A+a_][B+b_].., from upstream halfpics, e.g. (A)(a)(B)(b)...

 • 24pps.fromfile("source")hpps(Aa-Dd)(AaBbBcCdDd)]30'1.001htfps.tofile("target") creates an 'NTSC'-hard video.

In addition to pictures, scanpics & htpics are the streams that drive displays (after 1/(SAR)-scaling of course).
scanpics spps

<— scan (pseudo) pictures; scans interlaced at scanpics−per−second rate.

 • diagram elements: (Ab__)(Cd__) are 2 scanpics.

 • sfps.fromfile("source")[sps_spps.play plays without decombing.

 • sfps.fromfile("source")[sps(a)(aa)pps.play plays with bob decombing.

htpics htpps

<— hard telecined (pseudo) pictures; the following images at htpics−per−second rate:

13 ('PAL') or 3 ('NTSC') pictures  

<— halfpics decoded from ftframe fields, then interlaced.

plus 12 ('PAL') or 2 ('NTSC') combedpics

<— halfpics decoded from combedframe fields, then interlaced.

 • diagram elements: (Aa__)(Bb__)(Bc__)(Cd__)(Dd__) is a stride of 5 'NTSC' htpics with 2-3-2-3 pull-down.

Spframes & htpframes are not recommended due to combing effects, but both have been seen in the wild. Both are usually non-commercial or poorly mastered media.
spframes spfps

<— scan (pseudo) picture frames; scanpics frame-encoded at spframes−per−second rate.

 • diagram elements: [ab__][cd__] are 2 spframes.

 • sps]sfps encodes a downstream scanframe stream, e.g. [ab__][cd__].., from upstream scans, e.g. (a)(b)(c)(d)..; drops final orphan, if any.

htpframes htpfps

<— hard telecined (pseudo) picture frames; htpics frame-encoded at htpframes−per−second rate.

 • diagram elements: [Aa__][Bb__][Bc__][Cd__][Dd__] is a stride of 5 'NTSC' htpframes with 2-3-2-3 pull-down.

 • htpps]htpfps encodes a downstream htpframe stream, e.g. [Aa__][Bb__][Bc__][Cd__][Dd__], from upstream htpics, e.g. (Aa__)(Bb__)(Bc__)(Cd__)(Dd__).
